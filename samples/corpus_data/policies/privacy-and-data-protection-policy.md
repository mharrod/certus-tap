# Privacy & Data Protection Policy

## Purpose
Outline commitments to protect personal data, satisfy regulatory obligations (GDPR, CCPA), and support SOC 2 Privacy criteria.

## Principles
- **Lawfulness & Fairness:** Collect and process data only with lawful basis (consent, contract, legitimate interest).
- **Transparency:** Provide clear privacy notices describing processing activities, data retention, and user rights.
- **Data Minimization:** Ingest only necessary fields; pseudonymize or anonymize data when feasible.
- **Accuracy:** Implement workflows to correct inaccurate personal data promptly.
- **Security:** Apply encryption, access controls, and monitoring per classification policy.

## Data Subject Rights
- Support access, rectification, deletion, restriction, portability, and objection rights within regulatory timelines.
- Maintain request log, verification procedures, and communication templates.

## Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs)
- Conduct PIAs for new or changed processing of personal data, including ingestion of sensitive datasets or new surveillance tooling.
- Document risks, mitigations, and approvals before go-live.

## Third Parties
- Contracts must include data protection addenda, subprocessors lists, and breach notification terms.
- Require vendors to notify within 48 hours of confirmed incidents affecting our data.

## Governance
- Data Protection Officer (DPO) or delegate oversees compliance, training, and audit responses.
- Annual privacy training for all employees; specialized training for engineers handling Restricted data.

## Enforcement
Noncompliance may result in fines, contract penalties, and disciplinary action. All incidents involving personal data reported to Legal and DPO immediately.
